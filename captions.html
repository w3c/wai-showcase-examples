Web Accessibility Perspectives: Video Captions

Introduction

Web accessibility is essential for people with disabilities and useful for all. Learn about the impact of accessibility and the benefits for everyone in a variety of situations.

Video on Video Captions

[ Previous: Large Links, Buttons, and Controls | Up: Overview | Next: Customizable Text ]

What are Video Captions?

Captions are a text form of everything that is spoken in the audio as well as other important audio information that are otionally displayed on the video screen. This could include information about who is speaking if it is not visible, sound effects, music, laughter, etc. Captions are synchronized with the visual content. All audio played with visual content needs to be captioned, including video and animations.

Who depends on this feature?

  • People who are deaf and cannot hear the audio.
  • People who are hard of hearing and cannot hear some of the content.
  • People with cognitive and learning disabilities who need to see and hear the content to better understand it.

What are the additional benefits?

  • Content can be used in loud environments where you cannot hear the audio.
  • Content can be used in silent environments where you cannot use audio.
  • Content can be better understood by people hearing and seeing the information, for example people who are less fluent in the language spoken.
  • Content can be more easily transcribed using the captions, which also improves indexing for search engines.

What needs to happen for this to work?

Captions need to be made available with pre-recorded and live video content. Voice recognition software can help create captions for simple videos. Sometimes voice recognition software is built into the computer or social media platform, although automatically-generated captions often need editing. For many languages there are captioning services that are affordable.
