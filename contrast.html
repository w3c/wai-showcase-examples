Web Accessibility Perspectives: Colors with Good Contrast

Introduction

Web accessibility is essential for people with disabilities and useful for all. Learn about the impact of accessibility and the benefits for everyone in a variety of situations.

Video on Colors with Good Contrast

[ Previous: Keyboard Compatibility | Up: Overview | Next: Clear Layout and Design ]

What are Colors with Good Contrast?

There needs to be sufficient contrast between text color and its background (technically called luminosity contrast ratio). This includes text on images, icons, and buttons. Too little contrast makes the content difficult for some people to read. (While some people need high contrast, some people are sensitive to brightness and need to change the colors.)

Who depends on this feature?

  • People with color blindness who cannot distinguish between certain colours.
  • People with low contrast sensitivity, which is common in older people.

What are the additional benefits?

  • Content works in different lighting conditions, such as sunlight and glare, especially on mobile devices.
  • Content is easier to read by everyone, including those who do not have specific sight conditions.

What needs to happen for this to work?

Select foreground (text) and background colors that provide sufficient contrast. There are tools to help check and select appropriate color combinations. This is ideally done during the early design stage and the selection of color palettes.

Learn more

  • Accessibility Principle:
    • Content is easier to see and hear
  • Getting Started:
    • Provide sufficient contrast between foreground and background
  • Easy Check:
    • Contrast ratio ("color contrast")
  • User Story:
    • Mr. Lee, Online shopper with color blindness
    • Mr. Yunus, Retiree with low vision, hand tremor, and mild short-term memory loss

  • Mobile:
    • Information conveyed solely with color
