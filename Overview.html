Web Accessibility Perspectives: Explore the Impact and Benefits for Everyone

Web accessibility is essential for people with disabilities but useful for many more. Learn about some of the accessibility considerations and how they benefit everyone in different situations.

Keyboard Compatibility


Websites, including forms and applications, need to support keyboard interaction. This allows them to be usable by people using on-screen (touch), ergonomic, and specialized keyboards, as well as by voice recognition. This is essential for many people with physical and visual disabilities who may not be able to use a mouse. It is also useful in many other circumstances, such as for people with injuries such as a broken arm or repetitive stress injuries (RSI). It also allows websites to be usable on public terminals (kiosks) without a mouse or when the mouse simply breaks. Learn more about web accessibility - esential for some, useful for all.

Colors with Good Contrast


Many people have difficulty distinguishing certain colors, or perceive them differently. Ensuring adequate contrast between foreground and background colors of text, links, icons, and buttons allows websites to be usable by many more people. This is essential for people with disabilities, such as people with age-related contrast sensitivity. It is also useful for people in different circumstances, such as changing lighting conditions and glar, especially on mobile devices. Learn more about web accessibility - esential for some, useful for all.

Clear Layout and Design


Clear layout and design helps people navigate and find content more easily. Particularly website forms and applications can be unneccessarily complex and confusing. Yet clear layout and design is essential for people with low vision who may only be seeing a small portion of the screen at a time, and for people with cogntivie and learning disabilities who often need clarity and consistency to orient themselves on a website. It is also useful for many more, in particular to people who are new to the website or new to computers altogether. Also for people who may be viewing the website on a small computer screen, such as on a mobile device, or may be in a hurry to find the right information. Learn more about web accessibility - esential for some, useful for all.

Large Select Areas


Small links, buttons, checkboxes, and other form elements are difficult to use by almost everyone. This includes clicking them by mouse or tapping them on touch screens. Providing sufficent select areas is essential for people with reduced dexterity, such as physical disabilities and tremors due to age. It is also useful for people with injuries, such as repetitive stress injuries (RSI), and temporary limitiations, such as a bandaged arm. It is also allows the website to be usable on small screens, especially on mobile devices while moving around. Learn more about web accessibility - esential for some, useful for all.

Video Captions


Video captions are essential for people who are deaf and also for people who are hard of hearing and have trouble hearing certain audio. They are also used by people with cognitive and learning disabilities, such as some forms of dyselxia, to see and hear the content to better understand it. Captions are useful for people who are not fluent in the language, make video usable in loud and quite environments. They also support search-engine indexing, especially when they are accompanied by transcripts. Learn more about web accessibility - esential for some, useful for all.

Customizable Text


Being able to change the size, spacing, font, and colors of text is essential for many people with low vision and dyslexia. It is also useful for everyone who prefers particular presentation of text, especially for longer articles and content. This also allows content to reflow better on different window and screen sizes, and make it more usable across different devices. Text customization needs support by both the website and the web browser. Learn more about web accessibility - esential for some, useful for all.
